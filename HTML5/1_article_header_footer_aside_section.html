The Seed of an Idea

This tale symbolizes hope in the face of darkness.

In a small village, a boy named Kavi watched birds build a nest using twigs and threads. Inspired, he thought, “If birds can build homes without hands, why can't I create something useful too?” He began collecting broken tools, wires, and wood, dreaming of building a machine to help farmers in his village.

Everyone laughed at his strange idea, but Kavi kept working. Months later, he unveiled a simple machine that made planting seeds faster and easier. His idea, once ignored, changed many lives. From one small thought, a big difference grew—proving that great things often start with one simple idea.

Worried, he rushed to Doraemon, who explained that the future can always change. Inspired, Nobita shared the fruits with his friends and promised to stay close to them. With Doraemon's help, he began working harder—not just for success, but for happiness with the people he cared about.

One day, a stranger passed through town and gasped at Arin's wall. “Who painted this?” she cried, seeing what others couldn't. Slowly, more people began to see the colors too. Arin's belief had made the invisible visible. His idea changed the way the world saw beauty.
